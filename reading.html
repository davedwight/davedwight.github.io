NOVEMBER

The Unbearable Lightness of Being

A fascinating and lyrical novel about the Soviet occupation of Prague, love, and literally everything else. It's got some deep depths.

"The characters in my novels are my own unrealized possibilities. That is why I am equally fond of them all and equally horrified by them. Each one has crossed a border that I myself have circumvented. It is that crossed border (the border which my own "I" ends) attracts me the most. For beyond that border begins the secret the novel asks about. The novel is not the author's confession; it is an investigation of human life in the trap the world has become."

Turkey's Thirty Year Coup

The most comprehensive explanation that I have read of the forces contributing to the attempting coup in Turkey. While I found it particularly interesting because of its explanations for much of what happened this summer, it's also fantastic reporting on a covert, mysterious, and simply strange underground movement in a country that defies my understanding.

Deep Work

Imminently practical, pseudo-philosophical. This is a practical handbook to finding meaning. The result, if done well, is a perceived sense of meaning in your professional work - which is possible because of the depth of attention and concentration you devote to it. In my opinion, this method (deep work) won't solve the issue of "creeping nihilism" for all people unless it is directed at something higher. Newport argues that there is inherent meaning to deep work, to a craftsman, to deep concentration. I mostly agree. It allows you to get closer to the root of things, what is really going on, the thing itself. This book is about a method, but at times it pretends to also be about ends. It doesn't adequately answer where the inherent meaning to deep work comes from. It would be ridiculous to expect such answers from this type of book - it is only slightly irking that Newport pretends that he provides them here.
